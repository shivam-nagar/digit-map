About - Digit Map

The Numbers

Π - Of these three transcendental numbers, π is the most well known. It is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter (d = πr).
Φ - The Golden Ratio (φ) is the attractive proportion of values a and b (a > b) that satisfy (a+b)/a = a/b, which solves to a/b = (1+√5)/2.
e - The last of the three numbers, e is Euler's number and also known as the base of the natural logarithm.

Since, all the three are irrational numbers, the digits are random and never terminating. A color is assigned for each digit and nearest neighbours are linked till 2000 digits.
